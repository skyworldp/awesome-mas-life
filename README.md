## 🗺️ Overview

This companion repository supports the survey **Beyond Individual Intelligence: A Survey of Multi-agent Collaboration, Attribution and Evolution**, which studies the **full operational lifecycle** of LLM-based multi-agent systems (MAS): single-agent, multi-agent collaboration, multi-agent failure attribution, and multi-agent evolution that closes the loop from diagnosis to structural adaptation.

<p align="center">
   <img src="figs/teaser.png" alt="Survey overview figure" style="width: 100%;">
</p>

We organize the literature around **four main pillars**:

1. **Individual intelligence:** Reasoning, memory, planning, and tool use as modular capabilities of a single LLM-based agent.
2. **Multi-agent collaboration:** Roles, communication modes/protocols, orchestration topology, and interaction policies.
3. **Failure attribution:** Formal definitions, failure taxonomies, attribution methods, and evaluation benchmarks.
4. **Self-evolution:** From postmortem signals to updates at agentic, systemic, and meta levels (prompts, memory, parameters, topology, team composition, and whole-system design).
